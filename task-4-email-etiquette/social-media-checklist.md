Social Media "Ambassador" Checklist

Since our profiles are basically our digital resumes now, we have to be smart about what we leave behind. Here’s a quick gut-check for your accounts:

✅ The Do’s

The "Grandma" Test: Before you hit post, ask yourself if you’d be okay with your favorite professor or a future boss seeing it.

Google Yourself: Every now and then, search your name in an Incognito window to see exactly what a recruiter sees.

Share the Journey: Don’t just post the final certificate. Share a screenshot of a bug you finally fixed or a project you're working on.

Check Your Privacy: Set personal accounts to private, but keep LinkedIn and GitHub public so people can find your work.

❌ The Don’ts

Don’t Get Into "Keyboard Wars": It’s tempting to argue in the comments, but those screenshots live forever. Walk away from the trolls.

Don’t Overshare the "Where": Avoid posting your exact location or your hostel room number in real-time. Safety first!

Don’t Copy-Paste Everything: Give credit where it's due. Plagiarism is a quick way to lose professional credibility.

Don’t "Post and Ghost": If someone reaches out professionally, try to get back to them. Even a "No thank you" is better than ghosting.
