🛠️ My Progress

🎨 Visual Storytelling (Module 1)

I used Canva to turn complex ideas into a simple infographic. I focused on things we often overlook, like "password hygiene" and how to present ourselves online. It taught me that good communication is often about making things simple and visual.

💼 Building a Professional Brand (Module 2)

I’ve moved beyond just social media. I’ve now set up a LinkedIn for networking, GitHub for my future code, and Kaggle to explore data. These aren't just profiles; they are my digital resume for the next four years.

💻 Code & Collaboration (Module 3)

I tested my logic on HackerRank and learned how to gather community feedback using Google Forms. I realized that tools like Google Sheets are a lifesaver when you need to organize data for a group project.

Take my quiz here: https://forms.gle/ZnRajPa6GDMvpPCR8

📧 The Art of the Email (Module 4)

We’ve all sent "text-style" emails before. This module helped me fix that. I practiced writing to professors and recruiters, focusing on clarity and respect. I also put together a "Social Media Checklist" to make sure my digital footprint stays clean.


🛡️ Staying Cyber-Safe (Module 5)

This was the most eye-opening part. I researched how phishing works and realized how easy it is to fall for a "fake" UPI request. I’ve now committed to using 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) on everything and know exactly where to report trouble (1930).

🏁 Final Thoughts

This project wasn't just about passing a course; it was about getting "college-ready." I’m heading into the rest of my B.Tech feeling way more confident about how I show up online.
